Frequently Asked Questions

Wire-O binding can typically accommodate between 20 and 250 sheets (40-500 pages). The exact capacity depends on paper weight and wire diameter. For reference:

  • 20lb bond paper: up to 250 sheets
  • 28lb premium paper: up to 200 sheets
  • 70lb text weight: up to 150 sheets

For documents under 20 sheets, the binding may not be secure enough—consider saddle-stitch binding instead. For documents over 250 sheets, you'll need to split into multiple volumes or choose perfect binding.

Both allow pages to lay flat and rotate 360°, but there are key differences:

  • Wire-O: Uses twin metal loops that interlock. More professional appearance, slightly more durable, but can snag on fabric. Better for business documents and presentations.
  • Spiral Coil: Uses a single continuous plastic or metal coil. Less likely to snag, can hold slightly more pages, costs 10-20% less. Better for documents that will be carried frequently.

For most small business applications like reports and manuals, Wire-O provides the best balance of appearance and functionality.

We accept several formats, but PDF is strongly preferred:

  • Best: PDF/X-1a or PDF/X-4 with embedded fonts
  • Good: Standard PDF with fonts embedded and images at 300 DPI
  • Acceptable: Microsoft Word, PowerPoint (but we'll convert to PDF and may need to confirm formatting)

Make sure to use CMYK color mode (not RGB) and include 0.125" bleed on the top, bottom, and outside edges. Keep important content at least 0.5" away from the binding edge.
